ABOUT

"You get in life what you have the courage to ask for" -Oprah Winfrey

First of all, thank you for visiting my portfolio website! I am a college student in Tokyo, Japan. I was born and grew up here in Japan, and the only year I have lived outside this country was last year, when I was blessed with an opportunity to study abroad in the US for a year. I have been studying English since I was 1 year old baby, so I can say that my English is fluent enough to live abroad. I grew up in a same private girls school for 12 years, since primary school to high school. It was such a small community and I wanted to broaden my perspectives and horizons, so I decided to go to a different university, where all courses are taught in English and about 1/3 of the students are internationals. I believe that was one of the best life decisions I have ever made, as it made me realize how narrow my perspectives were, and it gave me a chance to broaden my world. EDUCATION

"Education is what remains after one has forgotten what one has learned in school." -Albert Einstein

Above is my favorite quote regarding education. I probably has forgotten all the things I learned and memorized in primary school to high school, but it has shaped the person I am today. A daily morning service I had at school has shaped the way I think. Teachers and friends I met has influenced me to a great extent. But what is important is to always keep in mind that the community you are in and you are influenced by, is a tiny tiny part of the world that you do not have to what everyone else in your community is doing. Since I went to the same school for twelve years, everyone was doing what other people was doing. If you stand out or try to follow a different path, people around you will spread rumors about you or warn you about all the risks you are taking. I am naturally adventurous and risk-taking person so it was sometimes hard for me. I always wanted to see the bigger world. With this in mind, I studied English like crazy so I can use my English ability to free myself from the teeny-tiny world I was in. When the time had come to think about college, I decided to take a differnet path that most of the people around me was taking, and to broaden my horizon by going to a bigger, better and more diverse university. That ended up as the best and first decision I had made in my life. At my university, it is mandatory to go study abroad for one whole year. I chose Carleton College (picture on the right) becuase it was middle of nowhere in Minnesota and they only accept one exchange student from all over the world. I wanted to go somewhere with less distraction, with closer and smaller community, and no other Japanese exchange students who I would feel confortable being with. I wanted to go out of my comfort zone. All people I met there was new people, with completely different backgrounds but I developed such a great friendships with them. They inspired me greatly, that it chnaged my life. They introduced me to a world of technology and Computer Science. I had never even paid attention to technology before but they influenced me so much that it became my dream to work in tech.
